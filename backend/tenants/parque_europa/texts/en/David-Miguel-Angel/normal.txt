David is one of the most famous sculptures of the Renaissance and a symbol of the city of Florence. Carved in marble by Michelangelo at the beginning of the sixteenth century, it represents the young biblical shepherd in the moment before the fight against Goliath. We do not see the fight scene, but rather the contained tension of the previous moment: the hero observes, calculates and decides.

The figure is recognized by its elegant posture, the so-called contrapposto, where the weight of the body rests on one leg while the other remains relaxed. This position creates a natural balance that gives life to the work. The realism of the muscles, hands and gaze reflects Michelangelo's technical mastery and the Renaissance's new confidence in the human being.

Throughout the centuries, David has been interpreted as a message of courage and intelligence: it is not brute force that wins, but cunning and determination. That is why it became a civic emblem for Florentines and, over time, an icon of universal culture.
