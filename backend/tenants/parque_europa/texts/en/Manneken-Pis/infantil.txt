
Hello little explorers! Today we are going to meet Manneken Pis, a very special statue that comes from Brussels, the capital of Belgium.

Do you know what "Manneken Pis" means? It means "urinating boy" in Flemish, which is one of the languages ​​spoken in Belgium. It's a very funny name!

This statue is very small, only sixty-one centimeters tall (like a big baby), but it is super famous all over the world. Millions of people go to see it every year! It's like the mascot of Brussels.

Do you know something very funny? Manneken Pis has a huge wardrobe, more than nine hundred outfits! They dress him according to the occasion: sometimes as a soccer player, other times as Santa Claus, as a soldier, as a chef... He's like a fashion doll!

The statue is made of bronze and represents a naked boy urinating in a fountain. Although it may seem strange, she is very loved by Brussels people. They find it very funny!

Do you know why it is so famous? Because it represents the sense of humor of the Belgians. Belgians like to laugh and not take things too seriously. Manneken Pis is a symbol of fun and joy!

The statue has many legends. One says that a boy saved the city from a fire by urinating on the fuse of a bomb. How brave! Another account that a lost child was found in that position. Whatever the case, it has become a very special symbol.

Would you like to see Manneken Pis dressed in something special? I'm sure you'd find it very funny! It's like a doll that changes clothes depending on the occasion.

Manneken Pis teaches us that sometimes the smallest things can be the most important. And it also reminds us that it's good to laugh and not worry too much about serious things. Life is more fun this way!

So enjoy looking at this special little boy. Maybe it will inspire you to be more fun and not take things too seriously. Laughter is very important!
